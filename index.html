Consumer Price Index (CPI) vs. Political Affiliation of Presidents Starting in 1981

Hypothesis: Consumer Price Index will be higher when a democratic president is in power.

Histogram of each presidential term vs the term average % change in CPI


Scatter plot of % change in CPI by year and political party in the white house

Conclusion: The data seems to show that CPI tends to increase more when Republicans are in control of the White House
which disproves my hypothesis.
